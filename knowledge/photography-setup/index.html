Camera

Â 

The Sony alpha 6000 is my main camera used for photography. I picked this mainly for it’s mirrorless, lightweight build. It has many features I’ve taken advantage of over the years, like it’s remote control app which is fantastic for long exposures with a tripod. I never shoot in RAW, only basic file formats as I don’t massively edit the complete photos in a conventional way, which you can read more about in my Trichrome post.

Software

I have used several different editing software over the years including Photoshop, Affinity, Gimp and Krita. As I’ve honed in on what my needs are for editing, I’ve realised that a lot of what I do when editing does not need massive editing suites. Eventually I hope to process my trichrome photography through various ImageMagick scripts, or some form of self-made tiny software solution.

Accessories

Â 

As my style of photography doesn’t require much, I haven’t acquired much in terms of accessories or tools for my camera setup. My tripod is an old, second-hand tripod that has a little wobble to it. My colour filters are hand made using cardboard, superglue and thin, coloured plastic sheets. These have served me very well, their imperfections in colour actually create the basis of my Trichrome technique, which I’m reluctant to change. In the future I might consider a solid backing for them as rain causes severe warping and fogging making them useless.
